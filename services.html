Training Without Pain Works!

Dog training in the United States is an unregulated industry, meaning there are no standardized educational or licensing requirements to practice professionally. By pursuing advanced education, reputable certifications, and ongoing continuing education, I am committed to maintaining a higher standard of professional excellence. My goal is to provide clients with knowledgeable, ethical, and evidence-based training while contributing to greater transparency and accountability within the field.

See below for the specific services I offer, which range from private in-person or virtual consults to in-person group classes. You can find more details and pricing information here.

Private Consultations

Private sessions typically start in your home so you can start learning how to communicate with your dog and teach them, free of distractions. We can focus on 1-2 behavior goals per session for basic manners, puppy behaviors, tricks, and adolescent challenges. Topics could include loose leash walking, house training, jumping, barking, coming when called, cooperative care with grooming, stays, puppy biting, crate training, door dashing, polite greetings, counter surfing, chewing, digging, tricks, and more.

Virtual private consultations

A virtual consultation (via Zoom or other platform) focused on 1-2 behaviors per session. Topics could include leash manners, house training, jumping, barking, recall, stays, puppy biting, crate training, door dashing, polite greetings, counter surfing, and more.

Group Classes

Group classes consist of 4-8 dogs for Puppy Playgroups, Puppy Preschool, Manners for Life (levels 1-3), and short workshops focused on either recall or leash manners. To get you and your dog off on the right paw, each class utilizes the latest knowledge about canine development and behavior to guide the course.

Pre-adoption prep

A 45-minute in-home consultation to help ensure you and your home are set up for success before your new puppy or dog arrives. This consult will help you avoid common mistakes around house training, barking, chewing, digging, counter surfing, play biting, separation anxiety, jumping, etc.

Fear, Reactivity, and Aggression

I specialize in fearful, reactive, and aggressive dogs. In each consultation, I educate you on the current science behind fearful barking, growling, lunging, nipping, & biting. We determine your dog's triggers and work to help your dog feel safe so they do not feel the need to defend themselves. I use counterconditioning and desensitization to change how your dog feels, which will change their behavior long term. I can help with resource guarding, dog-dog fighting, leash barking and lunging, chasing and nipping, fence fighting, window barking, stranger fears, etc.
